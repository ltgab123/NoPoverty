Facts About The SDG - 'No Poverty'


Key Aspects:

- Eradicate extreme poverty by lifting people earning less than $1.90 a day.
- Implement social protection systems to support those in need.
- Ensure equal rights to economic resources, access to basic services, ownership, and control over land and other forms of property.
- Reduce vulnerability to natural disasters and economic, social, or environmental shocks.
- Build resilience of the poor and those in vulnerable situations.

Indicators:

- Proportion of the population living below the national poverty line.
- Proportion of men, women, and children of all ages living in poverty in all its dimensions.
- Implementation of social protection systems and measures.
- Percentage of population covered by social protection floors/systems.

Importance:

- Addressing poverty is fundamental to achieving sustainable development.
- Poverty is interconnected with other SDGs, such as health, education, gender equality, and economic growth.

Global Context:

- Aims to significantly reduce poverty by 2030.
- Recognizes the multidimensional nature of poverty beyond income.

Facts About The Target 1.2

Target 1.2 of SDG 1 aims to halve, by 2030, the proportion of men, women, and children of all ages living in poverty in all its dimensions according to national definitions. This target emphasizes the need to address various dimensions of poverty, including income poverty, lack of access to basic resources and services, and social exclusion. By focusing on these dimensions, the goal is to ensure a comprehensive approach to poverty reduction that goes beyond just income measures. Achieving this target requires coordinated efforts at national and international levels to implement policies and programs that uplift the vulnerable and marginalized populations, fostering inclusive economic growth and social well-being.
