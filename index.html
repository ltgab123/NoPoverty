About Poverty

Poverty is a complex issue that affects millions of people worldwide. It is often defined as a lack of access to basic necessities such as food, shelter, education, and healthcare.

Factors contributing to poverty include unemployment, low wages, lack of education, discrimination, and limited access to resources. Poverty can have profound effects on individuals, families, and communities, leading to hunger, disease, and social unrest.

Poverty Facts

1. Over 700 million people worldwide live in extreme poverty, surviving on less than $1.90 per day.
2. Poverty disproportionately affects women and children, who are more vulnerable to malnutrition, disease, and exploitation.
3. Eradicating poverty requires addressing systemic issues such as inequality, lack of access to education, healthcare, and economic opportunities.
